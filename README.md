# macaque-embryo-atlas

Understanding the regulatory mechanisms of primate organogenesis represents a fundamental challenge in developmental biology. To address this, we constructed a single-cell gene regulatory atlas spanning early to late organogenesis in cynomolgus monkeys by integrating single-nucleus RNA sequencing (snRNA-seq) and single-nucleus ATAC sequencing (snATAC-seq) analyses. Our analyses reveal the dynamic heterogeneity, lineage trajectories, and key transcription factor (TF) networks driving the development of brain, macrophage, and trophoblast populations. Cross-species comparisons between humans and monkeys showed both conserved and species-specific features. Finally, this atlas was leveraged to link developmental disorder genes and disease-associated variants to their relevant embryonic cell types. Together, our data provide a foundational resource for understanding the regulatory basis of primate organogenesis and the origins of congenital disease.
